Spotless: Clean Phone & Photos — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 14, 2026

Spotless: Clean Phone & Photos (“the App”, “Spotless”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) is a storage cleaner and device-care app for iPhone. This privacy policy explains what data the App accesses, what we do and do not collect, and the choices you have. We built Spotless to be honest and privacy-first — so this policy is short and specific.

The short version. Your photos, videos, files, contacts, and calendar are analyzed entirely on your device. They are never uploaded to us or anyone else. The only data collected is anonymous, aggregated app-usage analytics used to fix bugs and improve the App.

1. Information we do NOT collect

The App’s core cleaning features run 100% on your iPhone. We do not collect, transmit, sell, or store any of the following:

  • Photos & videos — analyzed on-device (for duplicates, similar shots, blurry photos, screenshots, and large videos). They never leave your device.
  • Files you select for review.
  • Contacts and calendar events (if you use those cleanup tools) — read and processed on-device only.
  • Device metrics shown on the health dashboard (storage, battery status, thermal state, network status) — read on-device and not stored by us.

The App does not require an account, sign-in, or profile. We never ask for your name, address, or payment details.

2. Information collected automatically (analytics)

To understand which features are used and to diagnose crashes and bugs, the App uses Google Firebase Analytics. This collects a limited set of anonymous, aggregated technical data:

  • App events and interactions (e.g. which screens are opened, a scan is started, a purchase screen is shown)
  • Device model, iOS version, app version, language and coarse region
  • A pseudonymous app-instance identifier generated by Firebase
We do not use the Advertising Identifier (IDFA), we do not show ads, and we do not track you across other apps or websites. Analytics data cannot be used to identify you personally.

3. Permissions we request, and why

PermissionWhy the App asks
PhotosTo scan your library on-device for duplicates, similar shots, blurry photos, screenshots, and large videos, and to delete items you choose.
Contacts (optional)Only if you use the contact deduplication tool — to find and merge duplicates on-device.
Calendar (optional)Only if you use calendar cleanup — to find old past events on-device.
Notifications (optional)To send optional reminders (e.g. weekly cleanup reports or charging reminders).
Local NetworkOnly for the optional Wi-Fi speed test you run yourself.

You can review or revoke any permission at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security. Deleting a photo, video, or file in the App moves it to your Recently Deleted album (kept by iOS for 30 days) so nothing is lost by accident.

4. Purchases and subscriptions

Spotless offers optional auto-renewable subscriptions. All payments are processed by Apple through the App Store; we never receive or store your card or payment information. Subscription and receipt validation is handled by Apple’s StoreKit. See Apple’s Privacy Policy for how Apple handles purchase data.

5. Third-party services

The App relies on these third parties, each governed by its own privacy policy:

  • Google Firebase Analytics (analytics) — Firebase Privacy and Google Privacy Policy.
  • Apple (App Store distribution and payments) — Apple Privacy Policy.

We do not sell or share your personal data with any other third party.

6. Data retention

Because the App does not upload your content, we hold none of it. Anonymous analytics data is retained by Firebase according to Google’s standard retention settings and is used only in aggregate.

7. Children’s privacy

Spotless is not directed at children under 13 (or the minimum age in your country) and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

8. Your rights

Depending on where you live (for example under GDPR in the EU/UK or CCPA in California), you may have the right to access, correct, or delete personal data, and to object to certain processing. Because the App does not collect personal data that identifies you, most requests are satisfied by the fact that your content stays on your device. You can:

  • Revoke Photos/Contacts/Calendar/Notification permissions in iOS Settings at any time.
  • Opt out of analytics by removing the App.
  • Contact us with any privacy request at the address below.

9. Data security

Your content never leaves your device, which is the strongest protection possible. Any network requests the App makes (such as the optional speed test or analytics) use encrypted HTTPS connections.

10. International users

Anonymous analytics may be processed on servers operated by Google outside your country. By using the App you consent to this processing as described in Google’s and Firebase’s privacy policies linked above.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the “Effective date” above. Continued use of the App after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
